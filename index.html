the story

Where science meets product.

I started in a cardiac research lab studying how hearts fail — and somewhere between the bench and the bedside, I got obsessed with the systems that get people to care in the first place.


That led me to product management in digital health, where I've spent the last 7+ years building the kind of infrastructure that actually moves the needle — care navigation platforms, behavioral health analytics, patient engagement tools that work at scale.


I've done it at Aetna/CVS, at a scrappy EAP startup, and in the University of Minnesota's commercialization engine. What I care about: problems that are genuinely hard, teams that take craft seriously, and products where the stakes are real.

Access Plus — EAP Care Navigation Platform
Members were authorized for mental health care but couldn't get to it. The scheduling experience was fragmented, provider matching was manual, and digital engagement was nearly absent. I built the product that fixed it.

Resources for Living's EAP had a fundamental access problem: members were authorized for care but couldn't get to it fast enough. Scheduling was fragmented, provider matching was manual, and the digital experience hadn't kept pace.
  • Led discovery across clinical, operations, and account stakeholders to map the broken workflow and find the highest-leverage intervention points
  • Owned the full product roadmap — prioritizing scheduling and navigation first, then building automated engagement workflows on top
  • Launched 1,000+ automated monthly touchpoints that reduced unused authorizations and improved care utilization
  • Delivered in a HIPAA-regulated environment with enterprise reporting requirements layered on top

Aetna's behavioral health clients were making decisions with stale, inconsistent data. The opportunity: build a centralized analytics layer that surfaces real-time insights and makes them actionable.
